New Denver Restaurants

Olive & Finch - LINK 
The made-from-scratch breakfast, brunch, and pastry spot expands into the Golden Triangle, filling a neighborhood that has been screaming for a proper all-day anchor restaurant since roughly forever.

ZoiglBräu - LINK 
A German-influenced microbrewery rooted in the Zoigl cooperative brewing tradition out of Neuhaus, Bavaria, tapped its first keg this week in Aurora, bringing an old-world communal drinking ritual to the suburban Denver beer scene.

Ephemeral Tap & Bottle - LINK 
The rotating small-batch taproom and bottle shop opened a second Denver location in Jefferson Park this week, doubling down on a neighborhood that keeps earning it.

 

Developing Denver

Denver's Failing 16th Street Mall Is Getting the "Central Social District" Treatment - LINK 
A 10-person Urban Land Institute advisory panel told city leaders that the DDDA-owned Denver Pavilions should be transformed into a year-round community gathering hub with street-level retail, programming, and a massive public art installation on its blank five-story facade, giving the hollowed-out mall anchor something to do besides disappoint people.

South Federal Boulevard Is Next Up for Denver's Mixed-Use Push - LINK 
Plans filed this week would replace existing commercial properties along South Federal with 99 residential units and ground-floor retail, continuing Denver's aggressive infill push into historically commercial corridors that have sat underloved for decades.

Asia Center Owner Files Demolition Plans for Mixed-Use Redevelopment - LINK The owner of the Asia Center strip near the shuttered Denver Merchandise Mart submitted plans to demolish the retail block and replace it with a mixed-use development that would include affordable housing and new retail, adding another piece to Denver's ongoing effort to reclaim dead commercial zones.

Barcelona Wine Bar Is Bringing Spanish Tapas to the Denver Tech Center - LINK The Spanish wine and tapas concept is planting its flag at Marina Square in DTC, a signal of continued restaurant confidence in south Denver's expanding density and a welcome departure from the chain-restaurant monotony that has defined the corridor for years.

Denver's Own DNVR Sports Podcast Is Going National - LINK 
The DNVR Nuggets podcast, built from the ground up on fan-first community coverage and shared Colorado sports heartbreak, has partnered with the All City Network to expand nationally, making it one of the only local sports media success stories at a time when independent local coverage is collapsing almost everywhere else.

 

Denver News

Denver's $14.75 Million George Floyd Protest Verdict Is Not Going Anywhere - LINK 
A federal appeals court rejected Denver's attempt to toss the landmark civil rights ruling against the Denver Police Department, leaving the city responsible for more than $14 million stemming from officers' use of force against protesters in 2020.

Denver Is Draining an Entire Reservoir and It Is Because of the Drought - LINK Denver Water announced it will draw down Antero Reservoir for the first time in decades in response to critically low mountain snowpack, a move that signals how severe conditions have become as the city heads into summer with dwindling reserves.

State Air Regulators Are Demanding Public Hearings on the Data Center Elyria-Swansea Never Wanted - LINK 
Colorado air quality regulators are pushing for public meetings before the massive CoreSite data center project can advance in Elyria-Swansea, backing up residents who have spent months fighting an industrial development landing in one of Denver's most polluted and underserved neighborhoods.

A Brand New "Big Tent" Community Space Just Opened in a Downtown Office Nobody Was Using - LINK 
The Colorado People's Center opened this week on the 8th floor of a vacant downtown office building, creating a dedicated space for advocacy groups, mutual aid organizations, and public programming in the heart of a city that has been trying to figure out what to do with all its empty offices.

RTD's A Line Turned 10 and the Growing Pains Are Still Very Much Growing - LINK A decade after it debuted as Denver's bold transit experiment connecting downtown to DIA, the A Line remains a live case study in what happens when public infrastructure is rushed into service before it is ready, with some of the same mechanical and communication issues from its opening year still not fully resolved.

Denver's Only Lesbian Bar Is Closing Its Doors This Month - LINK 
The Pearl, which has operated out of the legendary Mercury Cafe space on California Street for the past year, announced it cannot continue, citing relentless operating costs, $60,000 in annual property taxes, and an operator who says Denver "conspires against anything interesting or unique right now."

Denver Builders Say Paying Workers Fair Wages Makes Affordable Housing Financially Impossible - LINK 
A new industry report reignites the long-running fight over whether requiring prevailing wages on city-subsidized affordable housing projects drives costs high enough to kill the projects entirely, putting Mayor Johnston's housing agenda in direct tension with organized labor in a city that desperately needs both.

Jokic Ran Across the Court to Confront a Timberwolf in Game 4 and Now the Nuggets Are One Loss From Done - LINK 
In the closing seconds of Game 4 in Minnesota, Nikola Jokic ran across the court to confront Jaden McDaniels after a tense playoff exchange, adding a dramatic exclamation point to a game the Nuggets lost, a series they now trail 1-3, and a night where Denver remembered what it feels like to watch a championship team look mortal.

Colorado Parks and Wildlife Just Had to Relocate a Moose From a Denver Neighborhood - LINK 
CPW officers spent part of their Sunday relocating a wayward moose that wandered into a Denver residential neighborhood this weekend, a reminder that spring in Colorado means wildlife does not respect city limits or your sense of calm.

Thieves Stole a Denver Family's Steel Sculpture Right Off Their Front Lawn After a Decade - LINK 
A Denver family is speaking out after someone made off with a large steel sculpture that had stood outside their home for more than ten years, proving that in this city nothing is too heavy, too personal, or too obviously someone else's property to walk off with.

Denver Public Schools Is About to Have a Very Public Fight Over How to Spend Its Budget - LINK 
DPS announced it will float several competing spending scenarios at a series of community meetings this spring, a process that will almost certainly put parents, teachers, and administrators in a room together and make absolutely everyone uncomfortable before it is over.

 

Denver Events

Lewis Capaldi at Red Rocks Amphitheatre - LINK 
Tuesday and Wednesday, April 28-29, Red Rocks Amphitheatre, Morrison.
After stepping away from touring following his Tourette syndrome diagnosis, Lewis Capaldi's return to the stage at one of the world's most beloved outdoor venues is either going to be the most cathartic thing that's happened at Red Rocks in years, or the moment you finally admit you cry at concerts.

Mile High Asian Food Week - LINK 
Through Sunday, May 3, across the Denver metro area.
Now in its fourth year, this week-long culinary celebration spans 73 Asian American, Native Hawaiian, and Pacific Islander-owned restaurants, food trucks, and pop-ups from Aurora to Colorado Springs, each offering secret menu items, exclusive discounts, and a reason to explore a corner of Denver's dining scene that doesn't get nearly enough ink.

Cinco de Mayo Festival at Civic Center Park - LINK 
Saturday and Sunday, May 2-3, Civic Center Park. Free admission.
One of the largest Cinco de Mayo festivals in the entire country comes home to Civic Center Park for two days of live music, food, and culture that routinely draws more than 300,000 people, turning downtown Denver into the loudest, most colorful version of itself for one full weekend.

Hadestown at the DCPA Wolf Theatre - LINK 
Opens Tuesday, May 5 (runs through May 10), Wolf Theatre at the Denver Performing Arts Complex.
The Tony Award-winning musical that weaves the myths of Orpheus, Eurydice, Hades, and Persephone into a Depression-era folk tragedy finally arrives in Denver for one week only, and if you have any feelings about love, loss, or a good banjo, you should be there.

Colorado Symphony: Stravinsky Rite of Spring with Kevin John Edusei - LINK Friday through Sunday, May 1-3, Boettcher Concert Hall.
The symphony's spring programming peaks this weekend with a three-night run of the piece that famously caused a riot at its 1913 Paris premiere, now performed with considerably less audience violence but no less visceral intensity in the world-class acoustics of Boettcher.

David Guetta at Red Rocks Amphitheatre - LINK 
Tuesday, May 5, Red Rocks Amphitheatre, Morrison.
The legendary DJ makes his first-ever Red Rocks appearance on a Tuesday night that has no business being this big, bringing the stadium-sized electronic energy of his decades-long career to the most spectacular outdoor room in the world.

Adventure Forest at the Children's Museum Opens for the Season - LINK Wednesday, May 6, Children's Museum of Denver at Marsico Campus.
Denver's beloved outdoor climbing course and immersive art installation at the Children's Museum reopens for the season, and if you think it's just for kids you have clearly never watched a grown adult try to get through the obstacle course while pretending they're fine.

Madama Butterfly at Opera Colorado - LINK 
Opens Saturday, May 2 (runs through May 10), Ellie Caulkins Opera House.
Puccini's most emotionally devastating opera returns to Opera Colorado for a limited run, telling the story of Cio-Cio San with the full-force heartbreak the composer intended, and giving Denver one of its few chances this spring to be completely wrecked in a formally sanctioned setting.

Colorado Plein Air Painters Spring Show at the Arvada Center - LINK 
Through Sunday, June 6, Arvada Center for the Arts and Humanities.
One of the largest juried shows in the state exclusively featuring Colorado artists runs through June with fresh outdoor paintings from across the state, now in its 35th year and still one of the best quiet afternoons you can have in the Denver metro.

Furry Scurry at Washington Park - LINK 
Saturday, May 2, Washington Park.
The 33rd annual Humane Colorado fundraiser brings thousands of dogs and their humans to Wash Park for a two-mile walk, a vendor festival, and enough collective goodwill to briefly make you forget that everything is a disaster, raising money for the 17,000-plus animals that cycle through the shelter system each year.

SeriesFest at Various Denver Venues - LINK 
Begins Thursday, May 7 (runs through May 15), various venues across Denver.
The award-winning television festival dedicated to showcasing bold episodic storytelling returns to Denver with screenings, Q&As, and industry panels across the city, built for the kind of person who has strong opinions about pilot episodes and likes to be right about them in public.

 

Weekly Denver Forecast

Denver is spending the last week of April dressed for late November and completely unwilling to apologize for it, with highs stuck in the low-to-mid 50s Monday through Friday and rain chances hovering between 35 and 45 percent through Thursday before the actual audacity of a snow chance arrives on Friday May 1. Pack a jacket, cancel your patio plans, and know that lows are dropping into the low-to-mid 40s overnight, which means the only people sleeping well this week are the ones who never put their winter gear away.

Denver Volunteer Opportunity

Humane Colorado, Furry Scurry Event Volunteer - LINK
With more than 17,000 animals cycling through Humane Colorado's shelters every year and less than one percent of their budget coming from government funding, every event dollar genuinely counts, and the 33rd annual Furry Scurry on Saturday May 2 at Washington Park needs volunteers for setup, registration, and teardown shifts ranging from two to four hours each. Sign up at furryscurry.org/volunteer and you will spend a Saturday morning surrounded by thousands of leashed dogs, which is either exactly what you need or a very good reason to show up anyway.

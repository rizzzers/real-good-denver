Got kiddos in high school or college?

There used to be a time when the path to a job looked pretty simple. Do your homework. Graduate. Learn Excel. Try not to accidentally reply all.

Unfortunately, the future has other plans.

The next generation of kids isn't just competing with other kids. They're competing with software that never sleeps, never complains, and can write a marketing plan before breakfast.

Which means something strange is happening in education. Being "good with computers" is no longer enough. The kids who will thrive are the ones who are AI native — the ones who know how to think with machines, not just type on them. Not in ten years. Right now.

So the real question for parents isn't screen time anymore. It's whether your kid knows how to collaborate with intelligence that lives inside a chat box.

This piece breaks down what parents can actually do about it without turning the house into a Silicon Valley lab experiment.

Let's go.

Denver News

Anti-vax dating app sues Denver beer garden for $4 million after canceling its singles mixer - LINK 
Unjected, the anti-vaccination dating platform, filed a federal discrimination lawsuit against Recess Beer Garden in LoHi after the venue booted the group's mixer event over escalating safety concerns, claiming the decision was driven by third-party pressure and cost the company millions.

Tina Peters walked out of prison Monday: Governor Polis sent her himself - LINK Convicted election denier and former Mesa County Clerk Tina Peters was released June 1 from La Vista Correctional Facility after Governor Jared Polis cut her nine-year sentence in half, a move condemned by Colorado's Secretary of State as an affront to democracy and cheered by allies of Donald Trump.

Denver City Councilmember Sarah Parady is resigning due to illness - LINK 
At-large councilmember Sarah Parady, one of Denver's most prominent progressive voices, announced Monday she will step down effective August 5 due to an undisclosed illness, leaving a significant vacancy on a council she helped define since 2023.

Denver homelessness is down 8%, but not everyone believes the city's count - LINK 
The 2026 Point-in-Time count shows an 8% regional drop in homelessness and a 21% decline in unsheltered individuals, but advocates say the numbers reflect increased policing pushing people into hiding rather than any real reduction in housing instability.

A youth was shot and killed at Montbello Recreation Center, which is now closed indefinitely - LINK 
The Montbello Recreation Center shuttered after a teenager was killed there, exposing deep cuts Mayor Johnston made to youth violence prevention funding in the 2026 budget while city officials insist a separate Broncos Fund more than covered the loss.

Colorado has 33,000 pounds of nuclear waste sitting outside Denver and no plan to move it - LINK 
As Colorado politicians dream of a nuclear energy revival, the state is quietly sitting on 15 tons of spent fuel rods stored in concrete vaults at the old Fort St. Vrain site north of Denver, with no permanent disposal option in sight and a growing movement to build even more reactors on the way.

Golf-ball hail slammed Denver Monday evening, denting cars and disrupting flights - LINK 
A fast-moving 10-minute hailstorm dropped golf-ball-sized ice across parts of the city June 1, sending residents scrambling for indoor parking and triggering a wave of insurance calls from Denverites who were not fast enough.

Denver City Council aides are organizing a union - LINK 
Staffers from across Denver City Council offices filed to unionize, citing inconsistent working conditions, high turnover, and a lack of formal grievance processes in a workplace where over 30 aides have left their jobs since 2023.

Community College of Denver held its first-ever commencement at Coors Field - LINK 
More than 600 Community College of Denver graduates crossed the stage at Coors Field on May 29, making it the first academic institution to hold its commencement ceremony at the Rockies' ballpark in what organizers called a landmark moment for accessible higher education in Denver.

Denver's migrant farm worker community is at the center of a national immigration debate - LINK 
A new report highlights how Colorado's agricultural workforce, largely composed of Mexican migrant laborers, is navigating escalating federal immigration enforcement as the state's food supply quietly depends on their continued presence.

Population growth in metro Denver is slowing, with some counties now actually shrinking - LINK 
New census data reveals that Colorado's historic population boom is cooling sharply, with several metro Denver counties seeing outright losses as housing costs, remote work reversals, and cost-of-living pressures push residents to less expensive states.

Denver Volunteer Opportunity

Denver Food Rescue, rescue and redistribute - LINK 
Denver Food Rescue uses bike power and a rotating volunteer crew to recover edible produce from grocery stores, farmers markets, and distributors that would otherwise be landfilled, then delivers it directly to 23 No Cost Grocery Program locations across the city. With food insecurity still impacting 1 in 9 Coloradans and recent city budget cuts to social services deepening the need, this is one of the most tangible hours you can give Denver. Shifts run 1–2 hours and are available most days of the week.

Developing Denver

Downtown's 16th Street Office Tower Is Becoming 120 Affordable Homes - LINK 
A concept plan has been resubmitted to convert the 12-story University Building at 910 16th Street into 120 income-restricted apartments targeting 60% AMI, using Denver's Proposition 123 fast-track approval process.

70 Affordable Units Are Being Squeezed Onto a Triangular Lot Near Coors Field - LINK 
A concept plan calls for an 8-story, 70-unit income-restricted building on a 0.36-acre triangular site at 29th Avenue and Fox Street, one of Denver's most transit-connected neighborhoods.

Colorado's Future Passenger Rail Now Has a Name, and 25,000 People Chose It - LINK 
The Front Range Passenger Rail District officially named the planned Fort Collins-to-Pueblo rail line "CoCo," short for Colorado Connector, after a statewide public vote, with early service targeted for 2029.

Downtown's Most Ambitious Office-to-Housing Conversion Gets $63 Million - LINK 
The Denver Downtown Development Authority approved its largest-ever loan to convert 1 million square feet of vacant office space at 621 and 633 17th Street into 700 apartments, a children's museum, bodega, bookstore, and rooftop pool.

Construction Kicks Off on a Major RiNo Transit-Oriented Development - LINK Construction is underway on AVE Station House in the RiNo Art District, a mixed-use transit-oriented project from a partnership of RXR, Korman Communities, and Invent Development Partners.

 

Denver News

Denver Zoo Paid $19 Million for a Sea Lion Exhibit That Is Already Leaking and Rusting - LINK 
Less than a year after opening, the Denver Zoological Foundation sued its architects and contractor over active water seepage, cracked beaches, rusting doors, and peeling paint throughout the $19 million Schlessman Shores habitat.

Denver's Only Lesbian Bar Is Closing, and the Circumstances Are a Lot - LINK 
The Pearl announced April will be its last month inside the legendary Mercury Cafe space, citing an inability to properly compensate staff, with a co-owner's alleged ties to an anti-LGBTQ church adding a surreal twist to the closure.

A Federal Judge Called Denver Public Schools "Conscience Shocking" Over the East High Shooting - LINK 
A federal judge ruled the lawsuit filed by former East High dean Eric Sinclair can proceed to trial, writing that DPS appeared to have "knowingly opened the door for a mass shooting" by ignoring documented warning signs.

200 Colorado Soldiers Left for the Middle East and Most of Denver Did Not Notice - LINK 
More than 200 soldiers from the Colorado Army National Guard's 1st Battalion, 157th Infantry Regiment deployed from Wings Over the Rockies on April 3 to the Sinai Peninsula, one month after a Fort Carson soldier was killed in Saudi Arabia.

Curious Theatre Company Sold Its Golden Triangle Home but Is Not Going Anywhere - LINK 
The beloved experimental theater company sold 1080 Acoma Street to developer David Spira, who plans a major renovation, while Curious relocates to Cleo Parker Robinson Dance's historic space for its 2026-2027 season.

Colorado Is Fast-Tracking Social Media Warrant Access After the Evergreen High School Shooting - LINK 
A Senate bill requiring platforms to respond to law enforcement warrants within 24 hours advanced through committee on emotional testimony from the Evergreen community, six months after a student shot two classmates inside the high school before taking his own life.

Anti-Trump Protests Are Still Happening in Denver, Even If Nobody Agrees Whether They Work - LINK 
Denver organizers from Solidarity Warriors to the weird-on-purpose Trash Heap Collective are debating the impact of 15 months of street protests after the No Kings 3 march drew millions nationally on March 28.

Someone Was Shot Dead During Road Rage in a Denver Dispensary Parking Lot at 2:30 AM - LINK 
Denver police cordoned off a block near Broadway and Maple Avenue after a road rage confrontation ended with one person dead and a black Ford Bronco at the center of the investigation.

Denver's Favorite Neighborhood Brewery Is Calling Last Round After 15 Years - LINK 
Copper Kettle Brewing Company, which won a Great American Beer Festival gold medal in its very first year, will pour its last pint on June 27, citing the economic pressure quietly gutting Denver's independent brewery scene.

Denver Lost Its Beloved Queer Bookstore This Week - LINK 
Petals and Pages, the queer woman-owned independent bookstore and community hub in the Art District on Santa Fe, held its final day on April 12 after three years of poetry nights and intimate literary events Denver cannot keep affording to lose.

The Cesar Chavez March in Denver Is Canceled After a Bombshell Abuse Investigation - LINK 
The Cesar Chavez Peace and Justice Committee of Denver canceled its annual April march after a New York Times investigation surfaced credible allegations of sexual abuse by the late labor icon, leaving the city to reckon with a complicated legacy on a street that bears his name.

Weekly Denver Forecast

Denver runs a 75-degree high on Thursday before Friday drops into the low 40s with a 45 percent chance of snow, which is the meteorological equivalent of a birthday cake followed immediately by a punch. The weekend recovers to the mid-50s Saturday with only a low chance of lingering snow, which in Denver's warped seasonal logic counts as a full recovery. With Colorado sitting deep in drought conditions, none of this sunshine is helping the snowpack, so enjoy the warmth and try not to think too hard about it.

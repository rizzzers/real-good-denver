New Denver Restaurants

Sundae - LINK 
Opened June 4, 1600 16th Street, Downtown Denver Colorado-born ice cream chain Sundae opened its sixth location on 16th Street last week, one of the first projects to receive financial support from the Downtown Development Authority, bringing house-made scoops and waffle cones to a stretch of downtown Denver that has been badly in need of a reason to stop and linger.

 

Developing Denver

Denver parks is cutting water use by 20 percent as drought bites - LINK 
With Gov. Polis declaring a statewide drought emergency, Denver Parks and Recreation is slashing irrigation at city parks by a fifth, a concrete policy response that shows the city is finally treating its water crisis like the emergency it is.

A new 358-Unit Transit-Oriented community just broke ground at 38th and Blake - LINK 
Formativ's Rowan apartment community at 3875 Walnut Street has officially begun construction, adding hundreds of homes within walking distance of a light rail station and advancing one of the city's most significant transit-oriented development sites.

King Soopers opens a massive new 103,000-square-foot grocery store in Denver - LINK 
The new King Soopers location celebrated its grand opening this week, giving a substantial chunk of the Denver metro a full-scale, modern grocery destination and signaling continued retail investment in the region despite broader economic headwinds.

A 33,000-Square-Foot nature library is almost finished in Thornton - LINK Thornton's nature-themed branch library is nearing completion, and when it opens it will stand as one of the most ambitious public library builds in the metro area in years, designed around the idea that civic space and natural learning belong in the same room.

The Denver Housing Authority is eyeing a massive redevelopment near Knox RTD station - LINK DHA is exploring the redevelopment of the Westridge and Mulroy properties near the Knox light rail station, a project that could bring transformative affordable housing density to a corridor that is ripe for transit-oriented growth.

 

Denver News

Colorado Court of Appeals Throws Out Convictions in Elijah McClain Case - LINK The two paramedics convicted of negligent homicide in the 2019 death of Elijah McClain will face new trials after a Colorado Court of Appeals decision reversed the jury verdicts, a ruling that lands like a gut punch for a family and a city that spent years fighting for any accountability at all.

Broncos Linebacker Jonathon Cooper Arrested on Domestic Violence Charges - LINK 
Cooper, who signed a four-year $60 million extension just last November, was arrested Friday morning in Douglas County on two counts of domestic violence after a dispute over cheating allegations at his apartment, with both him and his girlfriend booked and later released on bond.

Arvada Senior Apartment Complex Sued After Bedbug Infestation Spreads Unit to Unit - LINK 
A new lawsuit alleges that management at Heritage Apartments in Arvada knew about a bedbug infestation for months, watched it spread from apartment to apartment through an elderly population, and did nothing substantial to stop it.

Governor Polis Declares Statewide Drought Emergency - LINK 
Colorado's prolonged low snowpack and escalating dry conditions prompted Gov. Jared Polis to declare a formal statewide drought emergency, a milestone that underscores how severe the water situation has become heading into summer.

RTD Is Overhauling Downtown Denver Rail Service as the Downtown Rail Reconstruction Project Kicks Off - LINK 
RTD made significant changes to its light rail operations this week as the long-planned Downtown Rail Reconstruction Project moved into active construction, meaning riders on several lines can expect delays, reroutes, and service adjustments for the foreseeable future.

Westword Reporter Walks Colfax From the Capitol to Colorado Boulevard, and Here Is What He Found - LINK 
A Westword reporter spent a day walking one of the most storied, chaotic, and misunderstood streets in America end to end, logging conversations and observations from a stretch of city that manages to contain all of Denver's contradictions within a few miles.

Little Man Ice Cream Is Rebranding Three Beloved Denver Shops Under One Name - LINK 
Sweet Cooie's, Constellation, and Old Town Churn are all being absorbed into the Little Man Ice Cream brand, a consolidation that marks a major chapter in the story of one of Denver's most visually iconic and emotionally loaded dessert institutions.

Colorado GOP Gubernatorial Debate Turns Into a Brawl Over a Ministry Leader's Fitness for Office - LINK 
At a debate ahead of the Republican primary, candidates Barb Kirkmeyer and Scott Bottoms went after fellow contender Victor Marx directly, calling him "unfit" and "corrupt" in what became the sharpest intraparty fight of the 2026 governor's race so far.

Alamo Drafthouse Littleton Is Unionizing, and Organizers Say Management Tried to Spy on Them - LINK 
Workers at the Littleton location of the beloved dine-in cinema chain voted to unionize, and organizers are alleging that management fired supervisors for refusing to surveil union activity, turning a movie theater labor story into something far darker.

DEA Seizes 110,000 Fentanyl Pills in Major Denver-Area Bust - LINK 
Federal agents announced the seizure of more than 110,000 fentanyl-laced pills in the Denver area in a major DEA investigation, a haul that illustrates the scale at which distribution networks continue to operate despite ongoing enforcement pressure.

Bandimere Speedway Is Coming Down as a New Racetrack Takes Shape in Hudson - LINK 
Demolition has begun on one of Bandimere Speedway's most iconic buildings as the historic Colorado drag strip winds down its final days, with operators focused on building a new facility in Hudson that will carry the tradition forward.

Weekly Denver Forecast

Denver is entering summer with zero patience for moderation, with highs spiking into the low 90s Tuesday and Wednesday before a brief Thursday reprieve drops things back to a gentler 78 degrees, and Friday arriving dry and hot at 89. Lows are staying in the comfortable range, meaning mornings remain the only part of the day where a walk outside does not feel like a personal insult. Denver Parks is already cutting irrigation by 20 percent due to the statewide drought emergency, so enjoy that lush green lawn while it lasts.

Denver Volunteer Opportunity

Food Bank of the Rockies, Warehouse Volunteer - LINK 
With Gov.

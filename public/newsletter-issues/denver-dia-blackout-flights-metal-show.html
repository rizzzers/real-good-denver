New Denver Restaurants

Paperboy — LINK 
3940 W. 32nd Ave, West Highland. The Austin, Texas brunch phenomenon made its first-ever out-of-state leap to Denver's West Highland neighborhood, bringing Texas Hash, cheddar hashbrowns, cornmeal pancakes, and its notoriously Instagrammed chicken-and-biscuits with spicy honey.

Heretík — LINK 
1441 26th St, RiNo. James Beard semifinalist chef Theo Adley opened his long-awaited RiNo restaurant blending southern French and northeastern Spanish technique, anchored by a raw bar, heritage bird rotisserie, and the focused kind of menu that gets on best-of lists before the first month is out.

Fino — LINK 
3015 E. Colfax Ave, inside the All Inn Hotel. Set inside a $22 million restoration of a 1950s motor inn on Colfax, Fino leans into midcentury modern energy with olive leather booths and burlwood finishes, offering shared Mediterranean plates and cocktails in one of the most atmospheric new spaces to open on the avenue in years.

Risica — LINK 
3463 Walnut St, RiNo. Chef Andrea Frizzi returned to Denver after closing Il Post in 2024, opening a Milanese pizzeria and wine bar inside the new Edit building in RiNo, focused on brick-oven pies, Italian naturals, oysters, charcuterie, and the kind of clean room a serious pizza deserves.

BOXBOX Dining Room — LINK
5038 E. Colfax Ave, Hale. The Park Hill husband-and-wife operation expanded its bento-style salad concept with a full sit-down dining room, giving the neighborhood proper dine-in access to its rotating build-your-own grain bowl format with actual table service instead of a pickup counter.

 

Developing Denver

Affordable Housing Plus Daycare Is Heading to Green Valley Ranch — LINK 
Denver Health is pushing plans for a 156-unit affordable housing complex in the Gateway neighborhood that doubles as an early childhood education hub, a rare combo that actually makes sense.

That Northfield Mall Parking Lot Is Finally Getting a Purpose — LINK 
A developer wants to replace a chunk of Shops at Northfield's surface parking with a 210-unit duplex community designed around walkable open space, which is either a great idea or a parking nightmare waiting to happen.

Denver Is Using AI to Kill the Building Permit Backlog — LINK 
City Council advanced a $4.6 million, five-year contract with AI firm CivCheck to let software instantly flag errors in permit applications before they ever hit an overworked city staffer's desk.

Supportive Housing and Street-Level Retail Are Coming to Five Points — LINK 
Paragon Place, a new mixed-use supportive housing development, is moving forward at 2205 Washington Street, bringing both affordable units and activated street frontage to one of the city's most storied neighborhoods.

Summit FC's Sellout Just Lit a Fire Under Denver's Baker Stadium Dreams — LINK
After 63,000 fans descended on Mile High for the NWSL home opener, Baker residents and city planners are quietly imagining what a purpose-built women's soccer stadium would do to one of Denver's most underinvested neighborhoods.

 

Denver News

Denver Renames César Chávez Day to "Si Se Puede Day" Starting Today — LINK 
Mayor Johnston officially swapped the city's César Chávez holiday, removed the bust from Berkeley Park, and launched a community renaming process after a bombshell New York Times investigation detailed credible allegations of sexual abuse by the late labor icon.

Someone Hacked Colfax Crosswalk Signals to Broadcast Anti-Trump Messages — LINK 
Two pedestrian audio signals at East Colfax were rigged to scream profanity-laced anti-Trump messages in a robotic voice at unsuspecting pedestrians, and it was exactly as chaotic and hilarious as it sounds.

Denver's No Kings Protest Was the Biggest in Years, with 10,000 at the Capitol and 9 Arrests — LINK 
Saturday's third No Kings march drew an estimated 10,000 to the State Capitol, ended with police deploying pepper balls near an on-ramp, and organizers claim more than 8 million people protested nationally.

9News and Fox31 Are About to Share an Owner and Up to 90 Denver Journalists Could Pay the Price — LINK 
The Tegna-Nexstar merger got federal approval after 47 years of 9News independence, and while Kyle Clark is choked up on air, insiders predict a wave of layoffs that could gut local coverage across the market.

Denver Just Broke the All-Time March Heat Record. Twice. In One Week. — LINK Temperatures hit 87 degrees on March 25, surpassing a record from 1971, after already topping 85 on March 19, in a month when average highs should be hovering around 60 degrees.

Denver Airport TSA Workers Are Unpaid, Unbroken, and Furious — LINK 
DIA security agents are still showing up, smiling, and hitting 12-minute wait times during the 40-day DHS shutdown, but the union's regional VP says they are "falling apart inside" and some are still paying off debt from the shutdown before this one.

Denver Is Actually Shrinking While the Suburbs Keep Exploding — LINK 
New Census data shows Denver proper lost roughly 1,000 residents in 2025, the first decline in years, as Douglas County and the suburban fringe kept absorbing the growth the city used to capture.

Denver Summit FC Drew 63,004 Fans to the NWSL's Biggest Game in History and Still Couldn't Score — LINK 
Colorado's brand-new women's soccer team shattered the NWSL single-game attendance record by more than 22,000 in its very first home match, then played to a 0-0 draw with Washington Spirit, and the crowd absolutely did not care.

That Southeast Denver Parking Garage Didn't Just Crack, It Swallowed 20 Cars Underground — LINK 
A concrete support beam gave way at 3600 S. Yosemite Street, partially collapsing a two-story garage, trapping cars underground for days, and leaving one woman to describe watching cement land on her tire from three feet away.

Colorado's National Park Signs About Ute History and Pikas Are Being Flagged for Erasure — LINK 
A leaked federal database shows at least 10 items at Rocky Mountain and Mesa Verde have been flagged under Trump orders, including a sign about Ute ancestral land displacement and another about warming temperatures threatening pikas, the small mountain mammal that dies above 80 degrees.

The César Chávez March Was Canceled in Denver After a Devastating Exposé — LINK
The longtime Denver organizers behind the annual April march scrapped the event entirely after the New York Times published credible allegations that the late labor icon sexually abused teenage girls who worked alongside him.

Denver Volunteer Opportunity

Denver Food Rescue Opportunity — LINK
Food Bike Rescuer and No Cost Grocery Volunteer Denver Food Rescue recovers surplus fresh produce from grocery stores and wholesalers and delivers it entirely by e-bike directly to neighborhoods facing food insecurity, redistributing over 700,000 pounds of food annually without ever touching a warehouse.
Volunteers pick up a food rescue shift on their own schedule, claiming a pickup from a local business via an app and delivering it to a partner site, with most shifts taking under an hour. They also need consistent help staffing recurring No Cost Grocery distributions across several Denver neighborhoods.
Time Commitment: Fully flexible, from a single one-hour shift to a weekly recurring route.

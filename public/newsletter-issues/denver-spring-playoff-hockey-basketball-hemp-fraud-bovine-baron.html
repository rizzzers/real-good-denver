New Denver Restaurants

Colorado's first Drive-Thru-Only Chick-fil-A opens in Denver - LINK
Opened April 17, 9111 E. 40th Ave., Central Park Colorado's first drive-thru-only Chick-fil-A prototype debuted near I-70 and Central Park Boulevard with dedicated Mobile Thru lanes, walk-up windows, and zero dining room, a frictionless format built specifically for one of Denver's fastest-growing neighborhoods.

 

Developing Denver

DDDA Greenlights $63M Catalyst for Downtown Office-to-Residential Transformation - LINK 
The Denver Downtown Development Authority just approved $63 million to accelerate the conversion of vacant office towers into residential units, the city's boldest public bet yet on reversing downtown's post-pandemic emptiness and getting people actually living in the core.

AVE Station House Breaks Ground in RiNo as Transit-Oriented Living Hits Full Speed - LINK 
A partnership between RXR, Korman Communities, and Invent Development Partners has officially broken ground on AVE Station House, a mixed-use transit-adjacent residential development in the RiNo Arts District that puts residents steps from light rail and signals the neighborhood is far from done growing.

Denver City Council Introduces Yearlong Data Center Moratorium to Pump the Brakes on AI Infrastructure Sprawl - LINK 
With a massive CoreSite campus rising next to diesel generators in the already burdened Elyria-Swansea neighborhood and Denver Water calling for drought conservation, City Council dropped a one-year freeze on new data center construction to give the city time to write real rules before the AI buildout rewrites the map.

Vibrant Denver Bond Launches Public Tracker as City Unlocks $410M First Tranche for Neighborhoods - LINK 
Denver released an interactive public tracker showing exactly where voter-approved bond money is flowing after releasing its first $410 million tranche, covering park upgrades, recreation centers, sidewalk repairs, a skate park in southeast Denver, and a new American Indian Cultural Embassy.

Broe Group Kicks Off Phase Two of $200M Cherry Creek North Redevelopment - LINK 
The multi-billion-dollar Broe Group has launched the second phase of its $200 million overhaul of Cherry Creek North, converting surface parking near the creek into a walkable mixed-use district that is shaping up to be one of the most ambitious private bets on Denver's future in a decade.

 

Denver News

Adults Brawl at Lakewood Youth Baseball Over a Call by the 13-Year-Old Umpire - LINK 
Parents and spectators threw hands at a Lakewood youth baseball game over a disputed call made by a teenager, earning five citations, canceling the rest of both teams' seasons, and earning Denver a fresh national reputation for being constitutionally incapable of keeping it together at a children's sporting event.

Denver Jury Convicts Anti-Police Activist in First Case Under Colorado's Anti-Doxing Law - LINK 
A Denver jury found activist Regan Benson guilty of doxing a Denver police commander during a livestream, marking the first conviction under Colorado's new anti-doxing statute and establishing that broadcasting someone's name and badge number to a hostile audience online does in fact cross a legal line.

Colorado's 420 Festival Packed While the Weed Industry Posted Its Worst Numbers Since Legalization - LINK 
Thousands crowded Civic Center Park on Monday for Denver's annual 420 celebration even as state marijuana sales have cratered nearly 47% since their 2020 peak, the state's biggest dispensary chains are selling off or closing facilities, and the industry that made Colorado famous is quietly in a death spiral.

Colorado Marijuana Regulators Crack Down After Investigation Reveals Dispensaries Were Passing Off Hemp as Weed - LINK 
A joint ProPublica and Denver Gazette investigation found that some licensed Colorado dispensaries had been substituting cheaper, potentially hazardous hemp products for actual marijuana, robbing the state of millions in tax revenue, and state regulators responded with an emergency crackdown threatening license revocations.

Sheridan Teachers Have Been on Strike All Month and Now Democrats Want to Dissolve the District Into DPS - LINK 
After an entire month of striking teachers left Sheridan students functionally truant, a state senator is drafting legislation to absorb one of Colorado's poorest school districts into Denver Public Schools, a move the district's own superintendent says would effectively destroy a community built around its schools.

Federal Heights Police and Fire Chiefs Both Fired by Email on the Same Day - LINK Adams County DA Brian Mason publicly blasted Federal Heights city manager Jacqueline Halburnt after she fired both the police chief and the fire chief via email with no warning and no explanation, a move being described as one of the most abrupt dismissals in suburban Denver government memory.

Denver Metro Saw Three Dead and an Officer Stabbed in 24 Hours Last Thursday - LINK 
From a fatal shooting near Curtis Park to an Aurora officer stabbed multiple times in the head during a mental health call, the Denver metro recorded a brutal back-to-back stretch of violence last Thursday that left three people dead, a K9 stabbed, and the region unsettled heading into the weekend.

Colorado's Biggest Cannabis Chains Are Collapsing: Three Major Retailers in Freefall Inside One Month - LINK 
LivWell, Native Roots, and The Cannabist, three of Colorado's largest dispensary groups, have all been sold, sued, or scaled back within weeks of each other as wholesale pot prices have dropped more than 65% since 2021 and the pioneer of legal weed watches its biggest names fold under the pressure.

Denver Milk Chug-Off: The Bovine Baron Accepted the Challenge and He Absolutely Showed Up - LINK 
After pranksters plastered Observatory Park with flyers on April 1 daring a man known citywide as the Bovine Baron to a public milk chug-off, the Baron accepted without hesitation, showed up on time, and confirmed for the record that consuming large quantities of milk is, in fact, his whole deal.

Denver's New Civic Bookstore Has Barely Opened Its Doors and It Is Already in a Literary Fight - LINK 
The city's newly opened bookstore is already entangled in controversy, continuing the Denver literary community's remarkable streak of being completely incapable of going three months without a public dispute of some kind.

Drones and Undercover Cops Cut Crime in Curtis Park, but Residents Are Not Sure How They Feel About It - LINK 
Saturation patrols, undercover officers, and HALO drone surveillance have measurably reduced violent crime in the central Denver neighborhood, but residents say the trade-off comes with unresolved anxiety about sidewalks, homelessness, and what happens when the cameras move somewhere else.

Denver Volunteer Opportunity

Denver Food Rescue, E-Bike Food Recovery Volunteer - LINK 
With Denver Water calling for drought-driven conservation cuts and city budget reductions threatening social services across the board, Denver Food Rescue's e-bike fleet is one of the most direct connections between surplus groceries and neighbors facing real food insecurity right now. Volunteers claim a weekly recovery shift through the online portal, ride the route to a local grocer or market, and deliver fresh produce to community-run no-cost grocery sites, all in about two hours with no prior experience required. Attend a free virtual or in-person orientation with volunteer coordinators Max and Eve first to get scheduled.

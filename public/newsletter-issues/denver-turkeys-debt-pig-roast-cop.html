Denver News

"Pig Roast" Livestream Lands Denver Activist with Historic Doxing Conviction - LINK 
A Denver jury found activist Regan Benson guilty of doxing a police commander after she asked her 24,000 YouTube followers to find his home address and suggested hosting a "pig roast party" there, making her the first person ever convicted under Colorado's new anti-doxing law.

Colorado Appeals Court Upholds Tina Peters Conviction, Orders Resentencing - LINK 
The Colorado Court of Appeals affirmed the former Mesa County clerk's felony election tampering convictions while tossing her original nine-year sentence, ruling that the judge improperly punished her for her speech, sending the whole mess back to court.

Denver Is in Its Worst Drought on Record and Your Lawn Is Now Basically Illegal Two Days a Week - LINK 
Denver Water enacted its first mandatory Stage 1 drought restrictions since 2013, limiting 1.5 million customers to twice-weekly outdoor watering after Colorado snowpack hit the worst levels ever recorded in the utility's history.

Wild Turkeys Have Fully Taken Over Denver and Nobody Is Stopping Them - LINK Gobblers have been spotted strutting through downtown parking garages, perching on parked cars, and crossing busy intersections in Greenwood Village, and wildlife officials say it is only going to get worse through May mating season.

Judge Calls Denver Public Schools' Response to East High Shooter "Shocking Disregard" - LINK 
A judge allowed a lawsuit over the 2023 East High School shooting to move forward after ruling that Denver Public Schools appeared to have shown a shocking disregard for the risks posed by the shooter who was permitted to remain on campus.

This Is Denver's Ninth Marijuana Dispensary Recall of 2026 - LINK 
Another Colorado cannabis dispensary has been hit with a product recall, making it the ninth such action in Denver alone in just the first three months of the year, raising questions about quality control across the state's pot industry.

Jeffco Jail Hit With Yet Another Wrongful Death Lawsuit Over Medical Neglect - LINK A new lawsuit claims 40-year-old Courtney Tinker died at the Jefferson County jail after guards and a private medical contractor ignored her known lupus condition, the latest in a string of in-custody death cases targeting the facility.

Denver Assisted Living Facility Took 13 Minutes to Find a Collapsed Resident - LINK 
State investigators found that a Denver assisted living home took 13 full minutes to locate a resident who had collapsed and needed CPR, with regulators declaring that all 125 residents in the building were placed in immediate jeopardy.

Colorado's Snowpack Is So Low That Even a Record April Would Not Fix It - LINK Officials say it would take seven to eight feet of snow in April alone to restore normal water levels, an amount that has never happened in recorded history, meaning Denver is heading into summer already on the back foot.

Colorado GOP Limps Into Spring with $230K in Debt and $67K in the Bank - LINK New election filings reveal the Colorado Republican Party ended February more than $230,000 in debt with just $67,000 cash on hand, a financial hole that raises serious questions heading into a mid-decade election cycle.

Colorado Wildfire Building Codes Just Went Into Effect and Not Everyone Is Ready - LINK 
New statewide wildfire construction codes went live April 2, though local governments have until July 1 to implement them, creating an uneven patchwork of protections across the state heading into what threatens to be a brutal fire season.
